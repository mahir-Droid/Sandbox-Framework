	Black: Describes people of African or African diaspora descent.

	African American: Refers to Black people living in the United States, often with a history of African ancestry.

	African: Pertaining to the continent of Africa or people of African descent.

	Race: A concept that categorizes people based on shared physical or genetic characteristics.

	Ethnicity: Refers to a person's cultural identity, which can include aspects like language, traditions, and heritage.

	Minority: Describes a group with a smaller share of a population, often in the context of social and political power dynamics.

	Diversity: The presence of a variety of different racial and ethnic groups within a society or organization.

	Inclusion: The practice of ensuring that all individuals, regardless of their racial or ethnic background, are treated with respect and have equal opportunities.
